Advanced RISC Machines

The company that produced the [RISC](/terms/risc) processors used in Newton devices.

Early [MessagePad](/terms/messagepad) devices use a 20 MHz ARM 610 processor, whereas the [MessagePad 2000](/terms/messagepad-2000) and [MessagePad 2100](/terms/messagepad-2100) devices use a more powerful 160 MHz [StrongARM](/terms/strongarm) processor. The [eMate 300](/terms/emate-300) uses a 25 MHz ARM 710a processor.

The ARM architecture was developed by British computer manufacturer Acorn Computers and originally dubbed the Acorn RISC Machine.
