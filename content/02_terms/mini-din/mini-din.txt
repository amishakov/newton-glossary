Title: Mini-DIN

----

Date:

----

Text:

A standard [DIN](/terms/din) type of connector, generally used to connect peripherals such as mice, [modems](/terms/modem), and keyboards to computing devices.

The serial port found on a [Original MessagePad](/terms/original-messagepad), [MessagePad 100](/terms/messagepad-100), [MessagePad 110](/terms/messagepad-110), [MessagePad 120](/terms/messagepad-120), or [MessagePad 130](/terms/messagepad-130) device uses a [DIN-8](/terms/din-8) version of the Mini-DIN connector, so called because it has eight pins.

Newton peripherals such as the Newton Keyboard and [Newton Fax Modem](/terms/newton-fax-modem) also use the DIN-8 connector.

The [eMate 300](/terms/emate-300), the [Newton Serial Adapter](/terms/newton-serial-adapter), and the [GeoPort Telecom Adapter](/terms/geoport-telecom-adapter) use a [DIN-9](/terms/din-9) connector, which is compatible with the DIN-8 version, but has an additional pin to optionally provide power to an external device.
