Title: Boolean

----

Date: 24 May 2001

----

Text:

A special type of [immediate](/terms/immediate) value. In [NewtonScript](/terms/newtonscript), there is only one boolean, called `true`. [Functions](/terms/function) and control structures use [nil](/terms/nil) to represent `false`. When testing for a value, `nil` represents `false`, and any other value is equivalent to `true`.
