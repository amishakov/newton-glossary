Title: Hot Removal

----

Date: 25 October 2017

----

Text:

A feature pertaining to a device which allows the user to remove a (link: /terms/pc-card text: PC card) without having to power down the device. The (link: /terms/voyager text: Voyager) chipset used in the (link: /terms/messagepad-2000 text: MessagePad 2000), (link: /terms/messagepad-2100 text: MessagePad 2100), and (link: /terms/emate-300 text: eMate 300) includes a (link: /terms/pcmcia text: PCMCIA) controller which supports the ability to (link: /terms/hot-remove text: hot remove) PC cards.

The opposite of hot insertion is (link: /terms/hot-insertion text: hot insertion).
