Title: Voyager

----

Date: 24 May 2001

----

Text:

The name of the (link: /terms/asic text: ASIC) chipset developed by (link: /sources/cirrus-logic text: Cirrus Logic) and used in the (link: /terms/messagepad-2000 text: MessagePad 2000), (link: /terms/messagepad-2100 text: MessagePad 2100), and (link: /terms/emate-300 text: eMate 300) devices. Officially known as the (link: /terms/cl-ps7000 text: CL-PS7000), Voyager was this first chipset to support the (link: /terms/strongarm text: StrongARM) processor. It consisted of four chips which provided control for all digital subsystems including the, analogue peripherals, (link: /terms/pcmcia text: PCMCIA), and (link: /terms/infrared text: infrared) communication.

Voyager is sometimes referred to by the code name (link: /terms/cirrus text: Cirrus).
