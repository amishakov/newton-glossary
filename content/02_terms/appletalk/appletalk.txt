Title: AppleTalk

----

Date: 24 May 2001

----

Text:

A routable networking (link: /terms/protocol text: protocol) developed by [Apple](https://www.apple.com/) for communications between Mac OS computers and other platforms over a (link: /terms/local-area-network text: local area network). AppleTalk is a variation of (link: /terms/rs-422 text: RS-422) serial communication and is supported by physical networking such as (link: /terms/localtalk text: LocalTalk) (originally also called AppleTalk) and (link: /terms/ethernet text: Ethernet). AppleTalk over Ethernet in known as (link: /terms/ethertalk text: EtherTalk).

AppleTalk is also a section found in the (link: /terms/prefs text: Prefs) application.

In the AppleTalk (link: /terms/slip text: slip), the user can specify whether to connect to an network using LocalTalk or EtherTalk. When connecting using EtherTalk, a networking (link: /terms/pc-card text: PC card) can also be specified.

(image: /assets/images/appletalk-2x.gif alt: Screenshot of the AppleTalk preference slip width: 240)

AppleTalk appears in Prefs when the (link: /terms/newton-internet-enabler text: Newton Internet Enabler) software is installed.
