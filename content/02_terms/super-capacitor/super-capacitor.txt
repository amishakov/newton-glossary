Title: Super Capacitor

----

Date: 05 January 2025

----

Text:

An electronic component that can rapidly store and release an electrical charge, similar to a (link: /terms/battery text: battery).

(link: /terms/messagepad-2000 text: MessagePad 2000), (link: /terms/messagepad-2100 text: MessagePad 2100), and (link: /terms/emate-300 text: eMate 300) devices utilize a super capacitor to maintain (link: /terms/backup text: backup) power after removal of the (link: /terms/battery-pack text: battery pack). If left without power for a period of time longer than an hour, the super capacitor will deplete its charge and information such as the date and time will need to be reset on the device.

Rather than a super capacitor, other Newton devices use a removable (link: /terms/backup-battery text: backup battery).

Also referred to as a (link: /terms/supercap text: Supercap), (link: /terms/supercapacitor text: Supercapacitor), and (link: /terms/ultracapacitor text: Ultracapacitor).
