Title: Locking Tab

----

Date: 18 July 2024

----

Text:

A mechanism found on [Original MessagePad](/terms/original-messagepad), [MessagePad 100](/terms/messagepad-100), [MessagePad 110](/terms/messagepad-110), [MessagePad 110](/terms/messagepad-110), [MessagePad 120](/terms/messagepad-120), and [MessagePad 130](/terms/messagepad-130) devices which prevents the [PC card](/terms/pc-card) from being inadvertently ejected from the [card slot](/terms/card-slot).

Also referred to as a [Card Latch](/terms/card-latch).

The [Newton Internet Enabler Userâ€™s Guide](/sources/newton-internet-enabler-users-manual) erroneously refers to the locking tab as [card lock switch](/terms/card-lock-switch) which is actually a [write-protection switch](/terms/write-protection-switch) found on a [storage card](/terms/storage-card).

The button which releases the lid on an [eMate 300](/terms/emate-300) is also called a locking tab.
