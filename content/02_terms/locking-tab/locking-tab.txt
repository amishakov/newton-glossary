----

Date: 18 July 2024

----

Text:

A mechanism found on (link: /terms/original-messagepad text: Original MessagePad), (link: /terms/messagepad-100 text: MessagePad 100), (link: /terms/messagepad-110 text: MessagePad 110), (link: /terms/messagepad-110 text: MessagePad 110), (link: /terms/messagepad-120 text: MessagePad 120), and (link: /terms/messagepad-130 text: MessagePad 130) devices which prevents the (link: /terms/pc-card text: PC card) from being inadvertently ejected from the (link: /terms/card-slot text: card slot).

Also referred to as a (link: /terms/card-latch text: Card Latch) in the 1993 edition of the (link: /sources/messagepad-handbook-1993 text: MessagePad Handbook).

The (link: /sources/newton-internet-enabler-users-manual text: Newton Internet Enabler Userâ€™s Guide) erroneously refers to the locking tab as (link: /terms/card-lock-switch text: card lock switch) which is actually a (link: /terms/write-protection-switch text: write-protection switch) found on a (link: /terms/storage-card text: storage card).

The button which releases the lid on an (link: /terms/emate-300 text: eMate 300) is also called a locking tab.
