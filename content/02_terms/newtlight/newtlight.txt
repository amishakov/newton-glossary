Title: NewtLight

----

Date: 15 February 2025

----

Text:

A hardware accessory designed by (link: /sources/adriano-angelillis text: Adriano Angelillis) that provided a way to power (link: /terms/usb text: USB) devices using the serial port on a Newton device.

Several versions of the accessory were produced.

The (link: /terms/newtlight-8 text: NewtLight-8) utilized a (link: /terms/din-8 text: DIN-8) connector and was designed for use with all Newton devices. It could provide a maximum of 35 mA current to an external device.

The (link: /terms/newtlight-9 text: NewtLight-9) utilized a (link: /terms/din-9 text: DIN-9) connector, specifically for use with the (link: /terms/messagepad-2000 text: MessagePad 2000), (link: /terms/messagepad-2100 text: MessagePad 2100), and (link: /terms/emate-300 text: eMate 300). It could provide up to 100 mA of current to an external device.

The (link: /terms/newtlight-8m text: NewtLight-8M) and (link: /terms/newtlight-9m text: NewtLight-9M) also featured a magnetic base.

NewtLight is also the name of the software developed by (link: /sources/frank-grundel text: Frank Gr√ºndel) to control hardware accessories such as the (link: /terms/newteye text: NewtEye), (link: /terms/newtled text: NewtLED), and NewtLight.
